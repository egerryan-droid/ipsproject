## Executive Summary

The resource assessment catalogs IPS's tangible and intangible assets, evaluates their current condition, identifies trends, and assesses each resource's contribution to competitive positioning. Data sources include five years of audited financials (2021-2025), the IPS Finance Model V3, 30,241 customer invoices, 42,786 vendor AP records, the Vision/Traction Organizer, and leadership interviews conducted March 20 and March 25, 2026.

IPS's tangible resource base reflects a company in rapid growth that has not yet translated revenue growth into financial stability. Revenue grew 92% organically from $28M to $53.8M over three years with zero acquisitions. Total assets grew from $9.8M to $26.5M. However, the growth has consumed cash: the 2025 year-end cash position was negative $71K despite $5.2M in net income, driven by $6.3M in AR growth, $3.7M in owner distributions, and a 122-day DSO. The asset-light model (PP&E of only $943K on $54M in revenue) is both a strength (low fixed capital requirements) and a constraint (limited collateral for debt financing of the partner buyout).

The intangible resource base is where IPS's competitive differentiation resides --- and where the most significant risks concentrate. The Edwards/EST partnership, the longest-standing and most valuable vendor relationship, is actively eroding under new PE ownership. The brand and customer relationships are strong but underleveraged for recurring revenue. The leadership team's knowledge represents irreplaceable institutional capital that is neither codified nor succession-planned. Project Hub is potentially monetizable IP that has not been evaluated. Regulatory knowledge across multiple jurisdictions is a genuine barrier to competition that is entirely person-dependent.

The resource assessment reveals a company rich in relationship and knowledge assets, moderate in financial assets, and poor in the systems and infrastructure assets required to convert the first two categories into sustained competitive advantage.

#### Financial Resource Assessment

**Strengths:**
- Revenue trajectory is real and organic --- no acquisitions contributed to the $28M to $54M growth
- Gross margin expansion from 24.6% to 35.6% demonstrates operational improvement driven by the "parts and smarts" labor leverage model
- Conservative debt profile with only $6M in total obligations against $54M in revenue
- Asset-light model minimizes capital requirements and maximizes return on invested capital

**Vulnerabilities:**
- Negative cash position at year-end 2025 despite record revenue and earnings
- $3.7M in distributions from a cash-negative business signals a capital allocation problem
- DSO spiked to 122 days consuming $6.3M in working capital growth
- Backlog flat at $11.3M after 26% revenue growth raises pipeline replenishment questions
- Bad debt exploded from $9K to $849K (94x increase) --- either a material customer default or systemic collections deterioration

| Dimension | Assessment |

### Critical Finding: The Infrastructure Deficit

The resource assessment reveals a systematic pattern: IPS's relationship-based and knowledge-based resources are strong but fragile, while the systems and infrastructure resources needed to protect and monetize those relationship assets are weak or absent.

| Asset Category | Strength | Supporting Infrastructure | Gap |
|---------------|----------|--------------------------|-----|
| Customer relationships | 700-800 direct; 81.5% retention | CRM system | **Absent** |
| Fire engineering capability | NICET IV; $61.5M revenue | Training pipeline for NICET succession | **3-year goal** |
| Recurring revenue opportunity | 700-800 buildings accessible | NOC, helpdesk, subscription billing | **Absent** |
| Leadership knowledge | 17-24 year tenures | Succession plans, documented processes | **Absent** |
| AHJ relationships | 25+ years in 3 states | Relationship tracking system | **Absent** |
| Regulatory expertise | Multi-state, multi-code | Knowledge management system | **Absent** |

This infrastructure deficit is the central resource-level finding of the internal analysis. IPS is a company with $54M in revenue, $19M in gross profit, and $5.2M in net income that lacks the basic business infrastructure --- CRM, subscription billing, training programs, documented processes, succession plans --- that businesses one-quarter its size routinely operate.

The deficit is not a failure of vision. The VTO identifies most of these gaps explicitly. The deficit is a failure of prioritization: geographic expansion and revenue growth have consumed all available leadership bandwidth and capital, deferring the infrastructure investments that would make the growth sustainable and the company defensible.
