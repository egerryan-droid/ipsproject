## Executive Summary

The IPS Finance Model V3 is a sophisticated 43-sheet analytical tool projecting revenue from $53.8M to $100.6M by 2030 with EBITDA expanding from $5.8M to $22.5M. This pressure test validates the model's assumptions against actual operating data --- 30,241 customer invoices, 42,786 vendor AP records, five years of audited financials, and PE/EC consolidation intelligence --- to identify where assumptions hold, where they crack, and what the model entirely omits.

Three assumptions hold: the revenue trajectory to $82M by 2028 is plausible at 15% CAGR (though fragile due to concentration), direct labor leverage is real (34.5% to 24.9% of COGS), and total COGS percentage decline is audited (75.4% to 64.4%). Five assumptions crack under scrutiny: the COGS component split is distorted by a 2025 reclassification, DSO improvement to 75 days is structurally impossible as the EC channel grows to 43%, SGA decline to 21% contradicts 2025's 52% SGA growth, RMR cannot organically cross 10%, and $37M in acquisition costs are missing despite $28M in modeled acquisition revenue.

Most critically, the model entirely omits the most dangerous threat: PE-driven vertical integration of electrical contractors into fire and security. Three of IPS's eight EC customers are already PE or corporate-owned, ArchKey is actively building fire capability to displace IPS, and PEC Solutions revenue to IPS has already dropped 89%. This is not hypothetical --- it is documented and in progress.

## Section 1: Data Validation Results

### Test 1: COGS Trajectory --- PARTIALLY CRACKS

**Model assumes:** COGS declines from 64% to 56% of revenue over five years.

**What the data shows:**

| Year | Total COGS % | Direct Labor % | Materials+Sub % |
|------|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| 2021 | 75.4% | 34.5% | 28.0% |
| 2022 | 70.3% | 30.6% | 27.5% |
| 2023 | 70.1% | 30.0% | 28.2% |
| 2024 | 68.1% | 27.8% | 29.0% |
| 2025 | 64.4% | 24.9% | 31.5% |

Total COGS decline is real and audited. But the driver is direct labor leverage (34.5% to 24.9%), NOT materials/sub efficiency. Combined Materials plus Subcontract is actually INCREASING from 28.0% to 31.5%. A major reclassification occurred in 2025: Subcontract dropped from 6.8% to 1.6% while Materials jumped from 19.5% to 29.9% --- a $14M swing. Vendor AP shows subcontractor spend at 15.3% of revenue in 2025, not 1.6%.

Fire-through-EC average invoice grew from $11K to $37K --- larger projects through the lower-margin channel. Fire through EC represents 85% of all fire revenue in 2025, up from 69% in 2023.

**Verdict:** Total COGS trend holds. Component split is unreliable. Continued improvement depends on labor leverage outpacing the mix shift toward lower-margin EC fire projects. The 56% target requires both to work simultaneously.

### Test 2: DSO Trajectory --- CRACKS

**Model assumes:** DSO improves from 122 days to 75 days.

**What the data shows:**

| Channel | Payment Cycle | 2025 Rev Share |
|---------|--------------|---------------|
| EC/GC subcontract | 90-150 days | 43% |
| Amazon/National | 60-90 days | 24% |
| Direct end-user | 45-75 days | 15% |
| Service/recurring | 30-45 days | 18% |

Weighted blended DSO at current mix: approximately 85 days. At projected 50% EC share: approximately 91 days. Actual 2025 DSO of 122 days is worse than the weighted estimate, suggesting specific collection problems beyond mix.

**Verdict:** 75-day DSO is structurally impossible unless IPS shifts revenue mix away from EC channel --- the opposite of current trajectory.

---

### Test 3: SGA Spike --- CONTRADICTED

**Model assumes:** SGA declines from 25.1% to 21.4% by 2030.

**What the data shows:**

| Component | 2024-2025 Change | Nature |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| Salaries | +$2,533K (+54%) | **Structural** (hiring 120 to 140) |
| Bad debt | +$840K (94x) | Likely **one-time** |
| Health insurance | +$435K (+95%) | **Structural** (more employees) |
| Auto/lease | +$264K | **Structural** (more field vehicles) |
| Net structural | ~$3.6M | Does not reverse |

21% SGA requires revenue growing 87% while overhead grows 60%. 2025 showed overhead outpacing revenue. Realistic baseline: 24-25% for a company adding branches and headcount.

If SGA stays at 24.5%: cumulative EBITDA gap of $8.6M over 4 years. At 8x multiple: $70M less enterprise value.

**Verdict:** Contradicted. 24-25% SGA is realistic until IPS achieves genuine operating leverage through documented processes and technology --- neither of which currently exists.

---

### Test 4: RMR Trajectory --- OPTIMISTIC

**Model assumes:** RMR grows from 5.8% to 7.9% by 2030.

**What the data shows:**

| Category | 3-Year CAGR | Direction |
|----------|------------|-----------|
| Svc Agreements | 31.7% | Strong dollar growth |
| Monitoring (CSM) | 23.3% | Growing from tiny base |
| Test and Inspect | -14.1% | **Declining** despite mandates |
| Total recurring | 27.2% | Dollar growth |
| RMR as % of revenue | 6.9% to 5.8% | **Declining** (projects outpace) |

At actual growth rates (27% RMR, 15% revenue): crosses 10% around 2028. At model rates (25% declining to 15%): reaches only 7.9% by 2030.

Valuation gap at $82M revenue: $33-49M between 5.8% and 10% RMR.

**Verdict:** Dollar growth is real. Percentage cannot organically cross 10% because project revenue outpaces. The $33-49M valuation re-rating requires acquisition of a managed services firm, not organic growth.

---

### Test 5: Acquisition Gap --- INCONSISTENT

**Model includes:**
- M&A bolt-on revenue: $5M (2027), $5M (2028), $8M (2029), $10M (2030) = $28M at 30% margin

**Model omits:**
- Acquisition cost at 4x EBITDA: $5M revenue costs approximately $6.6M
- Total missing acquisition capex: approximately $37M over 2027-2030

**Corrected cash flow:**

| Scenario | 2030 Cash | Difference |
|----------|----------|------------|
| Model (no acquisition costs) | $50.8M | --- |
| Corrected (with acquisition costs) | $13.8M | -$37M |

**Verdict:** The model counts revenue it has not paid for. Largest single error. Corrected cash is still healthy but dramatically reduces buyout firepower.

---

### Test 6: Revenue to $82.4M by 2028 --- PLAUSIBLE BUT FRAGILE

15% CAGR from 2025-2028 is achievable given the 28% CAGR from 2023-2025. However, customer concentration is accelerating (top 3 at 46.4%), new customer acquisition is declining (220 to 173), and backlog is flat. Loss of one top-3 customer ($10-15M hit) breaks the trajectory. No diversification stress test exists in the model.

---

### Test 7: Gross Margin to 41% by 2028 --- NEEDS SCRUTINY

2025 actual: 35.6%. VTO target: 33%. Model exceeds VTO by 8 percentage points. Service mix shift would support margin expansion, but project revenue still dominates at 78.5%. The model's margin assumes a revenue mix shift that the data does not yet show happening.

---

### Test 8: Fire/Security Split --- SHIFTING

Fire grew 204% versus security access at 53% over three years. Fire is pulling ahead, increasing Edwards/Kidde dependency. The model should test: what happens if the Edwards partnership weakens under Lone Star Funds?

## Section 3: Scenario G --- Full Vertical Displacement with Fixed Cost Reality

### What Happens When EC Channel AND Amazon Are Both Lost

PE acquires IPS's top ECs and redirects fire work in-house. Amazon awards national contract to a PE platform. Revenue drops $31M by 2029.

**Critical correction:** Percentage-based models show EBITDA margins improving as low-margin revenue departs. Fixed-cost reality: 140 employees, 5 branches, 20 salespeople, and vehicle contracts do not flex with revenue.

### Fixed Cost Structure

| Category | Amount | Nature |
|----------|--------|--------|
| SGA fixed portion | $11.0M (82% of $13.5M) | Salaries, insurance, leases, IT |
| Direct labor | $13.4M | Semi-fixed (NICET techs cannot be rapidly reduced) |
| Variable costs | Materials, subs, commissions | These DO scale with revenue |

### P&L Under Fixed Cost Reality

| Year | Adj Revenue | Fixed Cost EBITDA | EBITDA Margin | vs. % Model |
|------|------------|------------------|--------------|------------|
| 2026 | $60.8M | $8.4M | 13.9% | Similar |
| 2027 | $72.0M | $12.3M | 17.1% | Similar |
| **2028** | **$64.4M** | **$8.2M** | **12.7%** | **$6.2M gap** |
| **2029** | **$61.2M** | **$7.5M** | **12.3%** | **$10.4M gap** |
| 2030 | $69.6M | $12.8M | 18.4% | Recovering |

**The Valley of Death: 2028-2029.** Revenue drops $18-31M. Labor costs barely move (5-15% cuts, lagged 6-12 months). EBITDA compresses to $7.5-8.2M. The percentage model showed $14.4-17.9M --- a $6-10M reality gap.

By 2030, after restructuring (20% labor reduction through attrition and selective cuts), EBITDA recovers to $12.8M at 18.4% margin on a higher-quality revenue base. The destination IS better. The path through 2028-2029 is the problem.

## Section 4: Scenario H --- Forced Buyout During Valley of Death

### The Cascading Failure

This is the scenario that threatens IPS's existence as an independent company.

Revenue declines (Scenario G) --> Partner (age 72 in 2028) sees value eroding, demands exit --> IPS has $3.5M FCF during valley --> Cannot fund buyout from operations --> Four outcomes, all bad.

### IPS Position When Partner Calls (2028)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Revenue | $64.4M (down 22% from base) |
| EBITDA | $8.2M (down 47% from base) |
| FCF | $3.5M/yr |
| Cash + LOC | ~$9-12M |

### 42% Stake Valuation Range

| Basis | Value |
|-------|-------|
| At current (depressed) | $26.1M |
| At peak (pre-decline) | $59.7M |
| Likely negotiation | $26-43M |

### The Four Outcomes

**Option A --- Negotiated Midpoint (~$43M):** Annual payment $7.2M. FCF $3.5M. Gap $3.7M/yr. Bridge: cut ALL distributions (+$3M) + max LOC (+$1.3M). Result: survivable but zero growth capital. Business treads water while PE eats the market.

**Option B --- Partner Demands Peak (~$60M):** Annual payment $10.1M. Max capacity $7.8M. Shortfall $2.2M/yr. Impossible without PE money.

**Option C --- Forced Sale to PE (Distressed):** PE offers 5x EBITDA (forced seller discount). EV: $40.8M. Equity: $37.8M. Leadership (58%): $21.9M. Partner (42%): $15.9M. Partner loses $43.9M versus peak.

**Option D --- PE Buys Partner Stake Directly:** PE offers partner $18.2M (15% premium to distressed). Partner accepts at age 72 for liquidity. PE now owns 42% of IPS. Board seat. Veto rights. Eventually forces full acquisition. **Leadership loses control without ever agreeing to sell.**

### The Killer Insight: Same Price, 3x the Capacity

| Dimension | Buy in 2026 | Buy in 2028 |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| 42% stake price | ~$27M | ~$27M |
| Annual payment (7yr/5%) | $4.6M | $4.6M |
| FCF available | **$10.0M** | **$3.5M** |
| Coverage ratio | **2.2x** | **0.76x** |
| Result | **Comfortable** | **Shortfall** |

The buyout costs the same. The ability to pay collapses by 65%.

### The Dual Clock

**Clock 1 (Biological):** Partner is 70 now. Every year adds urgency. By 72-73, patience is exhausted.

**Clock 2 (Strategic):** PE acquiring ECs and fire companies NOW. Revenue displacement begins 2027-2028.

**Convergence point:** 2027-2028. Window for funded buyout: NOW through early 2028.

**Strategic imperative:** BUYOUT FIRST (2026-2027), THEN PIVOT (2027-2029). While revenue is growing, FCF is strong, and the partner is patient. Price is the same. Capacity to pay is 3x higher. Full control enables the pivot. Reversing the order risks losing the company.
