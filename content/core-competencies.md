# Core Competencies — What IPS Does Distinctively

**Xavier EMBA Capstone | Internal Strategic Analysis | May 2026**
**Team: Choi, Eger, McKean, Peat, Tucker**

## Executive Summary

Core competency analysis identifies the capabilities that provide IPS with competitive advantage --- capabilities that are difficult to imitate, provide access to multiple markets, and make a significant contribution to perceived customer benefits. This analysis confirms three core competencies, identifies one emerging competency not yet operational, and flags four competency gaps representing the most significant barriers to strategic transformation.

The three confirmed core competencies are fire alarm system engineering and integration (the compliance-mandated anchor around which all other services layer), consultative multi-system integration across fire and security domains (the breadth that PE platforms pay 10-14x EBITDA to assemble), and local market relationship management (person-to-person trust networks built over 25-plus years that cannot be acquired through M&A). One emerging competency --- XaaS/cloud security platform delivery --- has technology partnerships in place but lacks organizational capability. This is IPS's most important capability gap.

Four competency gaps define the distance between IPS's current operating model and the model required to compete: recurring revenue operations, end-user relationship capture, formal training and knowledge management, and technology platform modernization. These gaps are interconnected and must be addressed as a system.

## Core Competency Framework

A core competency (Prahalad and Hamel, 1990) must satisfy three conditions:

| Condition | Test |
|-----------|------|
| **Access to Multiple Markets** | Does the competency enable participation in diverse segments? |
| **Customer Benefit Contribution** | Does it make a significant contribution to perceived customer value? |
| **Difficult to Imitate** | Is it genuinely hard for competitors to replicate? |

## Confirmed Core Competency 1: Fire Alarm System Engineering and Integration

### Cross-Framework Evidence

| Framework | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| **VRIO** | NICET IV rated V+R+I (Unused Competitive Advantage); $61.5M fire revenue, +204% |
| **Five Forces** | NICET IV + licensing + AHJ = "durable competitive moat" |
| **KSF** | Rated CRITICAL at 5/5 |
| **Value Net** | AHJ relationships: "the unacquirable moat" |
| **Strategic Groups** | "Single most important structural advantage" |
| **Conclusions** | "Fire alarm is the compliance-mandated anchor" |
| **Value Chain** | Highest-value activity retained in-house |

### Three-Condition Test

**Multiple Markets:** Healthcare ($9.7M), higher ed ($7.6M), K-12 ($4.2M), manufacturing ($4.9M), public/gov ($4.2M), data centers (77 planned in Columbus by 2030), logistics (Amazon, 48 states). Every commercial building requires fire alarm. Market-agnostic.

**Customer Value:** Code compliance (buildings shut down without it), life safety, insurance satisfaction, single-vendor accountability for most regulated building system, expert AHJ navigation. No building owner views this as optional.

**Imitation Difficulty:** NICET IV takes 10 years. Cannot be purchased. UL listing adds barriers. AHJ relationships are jurisdiction-specific and person-dependent. PE platforms can only acquire this by buying companies --- 129 fire/life safety deals in 2023, 109 through Sept 2024.

### Health Assessment

| Dimension | Rating | Evidence |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Current strength | **Strong** | $61.5M revenue; 204% growth; largest private Edwards distributor |
| Organizational support | **Partial** | No NICET pipeline; IPS University 3-year goal; succession gap |
| Sustainability | **At risk** | 85% fire revenue through EC at 1/5 defensibility |
| Scalability | **Limited** | Person-dependent; no documented processes |

**Strategic assessment:** Strongest core competency. Creates building access, drives trust, generates compliance-mandated stickiness. But 85% of fire revenue flows through ECs being acquired by PE. The APi Group model shows the path: inspection creates access, access creates service revenue, service creates recurring revenue, recurring revenue creates enterprise value. IPS has step one but not the rest.

## Confirmed Core Competency 2: Consultative Multi-System Integration

### Cross-Framework Evidence

| Framework | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| **VRIO** | Combined fire+security rated Temporary Advantage; 50/50 revenue split |
| **KSF** | 5/5 for IPS; Silco 4/5; cloud disruptors "locked out" of fire |
| **Value Net** | "IPS is an integration and orchestration business" |
| **Competitive Analysis** | "Three Uniques: Full-service, one-stop, consultative" |
| **Five Forces** | Bundling = "natural defense against entry" |

### Three-Condition Test

**Multiple Markets:** Dual-domain doubles addressable market. Healthcare, education, banking (national branch standardization), logistics, retail, government --- any vertical requiring both fire compliance and electronic security.

**Customer Value:** Single-vendor for most regulated (fire), most visible (video), and most critical (access) systems. Eliminates coordination complexity. Creates switching costs exceeding any individual service line.

**Imitation Difficulty:** Requires dual licensing per state, NICET, AHJ relationships, manufacturer authorization across both domains. Mobility barrier rated "Very High." PE achieves through acquisition but integration is complex.

### Health Assessment

| Dimension | Rating | Evidence |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Current strength | **Strong** | $129.1M combined over 3 years |
| Organizational support | **Moderate** | No cross-sell program; no CRM |
| Sustainability | **Moderate** | PE replicates via acquisition |
| Scalability | **Moderate** | National accounts prove execution |

## Confirmed Core Competency 3: Local Market Relationship Management

### Cross-Framework Evidence

| Framework | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| **VRIO** | Customer base rated Unused Advantage; 700-800 direct; 81.5% retention |
| **Strategic Groups** | "Customer loyalty built over decades"; Nashville local-hire model |
| **Competitive Analysis** | 25+ year relationships with Xavier, UC, Premier Health, Fifth Third |
| **Value Net** | AHJ trust, EC networks, CFMA presidency |

### Three-Condition Test

**Multiple Markets:** Access to institutional accounts, commercial accounts, government, and construction networks through referral and reputation effects.

**Customer Value:** Trusted local partner who knows specific buildings, AHJ relationships, code history, system configurations. Personalized expertise beyond technical capability.

**Imitation Difficulty:** 25+ year relationships built person-to-person. "PE platforms acquire customer lists and employee rosters; they do not automatically inherit the personal trust relationships."

### Health Assessment

| Dimension | Rating | Evidence |

| Organizational support | **Weak** | No CRM; no formal relationship management |
| Sustainability | **Moderate** | Person-dependent; 18.5% annual churn |

## Competency Gap 1: Recurring Revenue Operations

No infrastructure to deliver, bill, or manage recurring services at scale.

| Component | Current | Required |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| NOC | Third-party resale ($48.5K/mo) | Owned or white-labeled 24/7 |
| Helpdesk | None | Credential mgmt, alarm response |
| Subscription billing | None | Automated with dunning |
| SLAs | Informal; no MSAs | Standardized by tier |
| Customer success | None | Proactive retention/expansion |

**Impact:** RMR stays at 5.8%. $33-49M valuation gap. $14-21M buyout impact on 42% stake.

## How Competencies and Gaps Connect

### The Virtuous Cycle (When Working)

Fire engineering creates building access. Access enables consultative integration. Integration deepens local relationships. Relationships generate repeat business.

### Where the Cycle Breaks

After installation, IPS walks away. Building access is not converted to recurring relationship. Integration is not followed by managed services. Relationship is not deepened by monthly delivery. The cycle becomes a value leak.

### Sequencing

Technology platform first (CRM + billing, 6 months) --> end-user capture (concurrent) --> recurring revenue operations (12 months) --> formalized training (18-24 months). Or: acquire a managed security firm to collapse gaps 1-3 into one transaction.
