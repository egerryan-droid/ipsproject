## Executive Summary

The operating model that built IPS cannot sustain it in the environment that now exists. IPS does not have a growth engine. It has a geography engine. The company enters markets, rides the construction cycle, farms the relationships the cycle creates, and when those relationships age out at 18.5% per year, it needs the next market. That treadmill worked when IPS was entering uncontested markets. Every market worth entering is now pre-contested by PE-backed platforms that offer more services, more scale, and a subscription model that locks in the customer IPS would be trying to farm.

The individual components --- NICET IV capability, customer relationships, technical execution quality --- are genuinely excellent. But they are housed in an operating model that gives away the recurring value, cannot grow without new geography, and has no mechanism to defend itself against competitors specifically designed to exploit these gaps.

This thesis is not "here are three ways to grow revenue." This is: "the operating model that built this company cannot sustain it in the environment that now exists, and here is the evidence, and here is what replaces it." The thesis has been tested against leadership's own words from the March 20, 2026 strategy meeting and the March 25 CFO debrief. It confirms 8 for 8 --- every element of the thesis is supported by what leadership said, what leadership did not say, and what the financial data reveals.

## The Three Recommendations

Not growth tactics. Business model transformation.

### 1. Stop Leaving the Building

Convert every installation into an ongoing relationship. Monitoring, inspection contracts, managed services. The fire technician is already through the door. Make the monthly invoice part of every project close, not a separate sale that never happens.

**What this requires:**
- Subscription billing infrastructure
- NOC or white-labeled monitoring capability
- Helpdesk for credential management and system health
- Service agreement template at every project close
- Sales compensation restructured to reward recurring revenue

**Why it matters:** At a 15% monitoring attach rate on 700-800 direct building owners at $500/month, IPS generates $630-720K in new annual recurring revenue --- more than doubling the current $582K monitoring book. At 50% margins, this adds approximately $315-360K in annual gross profit from existing relationships with no new customer acquisition cost.

### 2. Build or Buy the Delivery Capability

NOC, helpdesk, subscription billing, managed services delivery. Either acquire a small managed security firm ($3-5M) or partner with one. Without delivery infrastructure, the monthly invoice is a promise you cannot keep.

**Build path:** 2-3 staff for helpdesk, subscription billing software, Brivo/Genetec remote management platform. Timeline: 12-18 months. Cost: $500K-$1M annually.

**Buy path:** Acquire a $3-5M managed security firm. Immediately gains NOC, helpdesk, billing infrastructure, and an existing RMR book. Timeline: 6-12 months. Cost: $6-10M at 4x EBITDA. Faster, proven, but requires capital.

### 3. Redesign Sales Compensation

Reward recurring revenue, not just project closes. Until the incentive structure changes, the behavior will not change. A salesperson who earns commission on a $200K project and nothing on a $500/month monitoring contract will sell the project every time.

**Implementation:** Add RMR commission at 3-5x the monthly value as a one-time bonus. Add service agreement attachment as a KPI in quarterly reviews. Create a dedicated service sales role separate from project sales. Report RMR metrics in weekly branch meetings.

## The Order: Buyout First, Then Pivot, Then Build

The sequencing of strategic moves is as important as the moves themselves.

### Why Buyout First

The partner buyout must happen while revenue is growing and FCF is strong (2026-2027). The business model pivot causes a revenue mix shift --- reducing EC-channel project revenue while growing direct recurring revenue. During the transition, total revenue may flatten or dip. If the 42% silent partner (age 70) sees revenue declining and value eroding, he may demand exit at exactly the moment IPS's ability to pay is weakest.

### The Math

| Dimension | Buy in 2026 | Buy in 2028 |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| 42% stake price | ~$27M | ~$27M |
| Annual payment (7yr/5%) | $4.6M | $4.6M |
| FCF available | $10.0M | $3.5M |
| Coverage ratio | 2.2x | 0.76x |
| Result | Comfortable | Shortfall |

The buyout costs the same regardless of timing. The ability to pay collapses by 65% if delayed to 2028. Full ownership control enables the pivot. Reversing the order risks losing the company to a forced sale or hostile minority acquisition.

### The Dual Clock

**Clock 1 (Biological):** Partner is 70 now. By 72-73, patience for exit is exhausted.

**Clock 2 (Strategic):** PE acquiring ECs and fire companies now. Revenue displacement begins 2027-2028. Convergent already in Cincinnati. Pye-Barker already in Cincinnati. ArchKey building fire team.

**Convergence:** 2027-2028. Both clocks expire simultaneously. The window for a funded buyout is now through early 2028.

## What This Thesis Is Not

This is not "here are three ways to grow revenue."

This is not pessimism about IPS's capability. The NICET IV engineering, the 25-year customer relationships, the consultative multi-system integration --- these are genuinely excellent. IPS built a $55M business from $5M organically over 17 years with zero acquisitions. That is a real achievement reflecting real capability.

This thesis says: the operating model that produced that achievement cannot sustain the company in the competitive environment that now exists. The environment changed. The model did not. The recommendations are not about growing faster. They are about growing differently.

## The Recommended Sequence

### Phase 1: Buyout (2026-2027)

Execute the partner buyout while revenue is growing, FCF is strong, and the partner is patient. Price: approximately $27M on the 42% stake. Annual payment: $4.6M on $10M FCF. Coverage ratio: 2.2x. Comfortable.

### Phase 2: Pivot (2027-2029)

With full ownership control, transform the revenue model:
- Deploy CRM and subscription billing (months 1-6)
- Restructure sales compensation (month 3)
- Launch managed services (months 6-12) via build or acquire
- Systematically capture building owner relationships on EC projects
- Begin reducing EC channel dependency through direct-to-owner fire service

### Phase 3: Build (2029-2031)

Scale the new model:
- Formalize IPS University with NICET certification track
- Document all core processes and SOPs
- Codify the Nashville branch launch playbook
- Expand to Atlanta and beyond with the new model --- not the old one
- Develop secondary fire alarm OEM competency (Notifier or Hochiki)
- Build or partner for cybersecurity capability per NFPA 72-2025
